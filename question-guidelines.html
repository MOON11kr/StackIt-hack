Question Guidelines

Learn how to write effective questions that get answers from our community of developers and experts.

How to Write a Good Question

  • Search First

    Before asking, search to see if your question has already been answered. Many common questions have been asked before.

  • Be Specific

    Your title should summarize the specific problem you're facing, not your general goal.

    Bad Example

    "Help with React code"

    Good Example

    "Why does my React component re-render when passing props that haven't changed?"

  • Provide Details

    Include relevant code, error messages, and what you've already tried. The more context you provide, the better answers you'll get.

  • Tag Appropriately

    Use relevant tags to help experts find your question. Don't use more than 5 tags, and avoid creating new tags unless necessary.

What to Avoid

  • Don't Ask Opinion-Based Questions

    Questions like "What's the best framework?" typically lead to debates rather than answers.

  • Don't Post Screenshots of Code

    Always post the actual code as text so others can copy/paste and test it.

  • No Solicitation

    Don't ask people to contact you outside the site or offer to pay for answers.

Additional Tips

  • Check Grammar and Spelling

    Well-written questions are easier to understand and more likely to get answers.

  • Be Patient

    Good answers often take time. Don't bump your question repeatedly.

  • Accept Answers

    When you get a helpful answer, accept it to mark the question as resolved.
